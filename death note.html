DEATH NOTE


Action, Adventure, Fantasy, Shonen

Death Note is a psychological thriller anime and manga series written by Tsugumi Ohba and illustrated by Takeshi Obata. The story follows Light Yagami, a highly intelligent but disillusioned high school student who discovers a mysterious notebook called the Death Note. This supernatural notebook grants its user the ability to kill anyone by simply writing their name in it, as long as they know the person's face


As Light begins his reign of terror, his actions catch the attention of law enforcement agencies worldwide. In response, the enigmatic and genius detective known only as L takes on the challenge of capturing Kira. This leads to a gripping battle of wits, as Light and L engage in a high-stakes cat-and-mouse game, each trying to outmaneuver the other. The tension escalates as more characters get involved, including Misa Amane.


The series explores deep themes of justice, morality, power, and the consequences of absolute authority. With only 37 episodes, Death Note is praised for its intelligent storytelling, psychological depth, and unpredictable plot twists. It remains one of the most critically acclaimed anime series, leaving a lasting impact on viewers with its dark and thought-provoking narrative.
